Shikamaru was under high pressure from the Feudal Lord and needed everything to go perfectly. He had just been informed by Kashin Koji that the village would soon be attacked by Bug, one of the Divine Tree clones, meaning the village's top Jonin would be completely occupied.

Amidst this chaos, he received an anonymous tip that the Feudal Lord’s son was going to be abducted during a trade trip to the Land of Mist. The culprit was a member of the Hyuga clan whom they had been secretly investigating under suspicion of being a hired merchant-assassin. With such a dangerous threat at hand, Shikamaru needed to assign this mission to a ninja competent enough to best a Hyuga while he managed the village against Bug's impending invasion.

Inside a house, a big guy in a hood rested against a dog’s back with a bottle of beer in his hand. Kiba was laying on the floor with a dim look on his face. Nearby, a woman knitting wool looked up.

"Lord Hokage, what brings you to our home?" she said to Shikamaru.

"I have urgent matters I need your husband for," he replied.

"My husband? What good is a drunk to you?"

"There isn't a more competent ninja in the village at your husband's specialty."

"If getting drunk at these early hours of the morning was a skill, then I must agree, Lord Hokage."

"Shut up, woman." Kiba spoke up, glancing over. "Hey, Shikamaru, didn't you get my letter? I'm retired. I have no business with the Hokage."

Shikamaru looked toward Kiba, who didn't look like he was going to give in. "I will have a team of Chunin ready to go. If they are to be successful, they will need your guidance."

With that, Shikamaru took his leave.

Later, Lizzy—Kiba’s wife—was serving lunch for the dog, herself, and Kiba. Kiba dragged himself to the table and fell heavily into his seat. She looked at him for a while before speaking.

"I think you should go. The village needs you—that’s why the Hokage came personally."

"Tough luck. I'm a wine seller now. If the village isn't looking to buy wine, then I have nothing to offer."

"Wine seller, my foot. You drink more wine than you sell."

"I just want to eat in peace. Will you stop being a drag?"

"I'm sure the Sixth and Seventh Hokage would think..."

"The Seventh Hokage wouldn't stand with his tail between his legs while the Divine Trees are running riot in the village, would he now?!" Kiba snapped.

Lizzy stared at him. "I got married to the bravest and most optimistic ninja in the village—not the strongest. I don't even know what I'm looking at right now." She stood up and walked out of the room.

Kiba glared toward the door, then threw his bottle hard against the wall. "Shit!"

Houki Taketori and Iwabe stood near the village gate, waiting. Denki arrived shortly after, looking winded. "You guys also got the message from the Hokage?"

Houki looked at him. "I was briefed on the mission by the ANBU. We are supposed to wait for one final member before we get started. If he doesn't arrive before sunset, we will have to leave without him, and I will be running the mission."

The sky gradually darkened until the sun finally dipped below the horizon.

"It's sunset. Let's go," Houki said, adjusting his vest. "We can't risk anyone eavesdropping, so I will brief you guys once we are a reasonable distance away from the village."

They blitzed away, instantly blurring into the forest and jumping rapidly through the trees.

"We could have waited a little longer for the last member," Denki called out over the rushing wind.

"A ninja who can't show up on time is just a burden who would slow us down anyway," Iwabe grunted, keeping pace.

Houki glanced back at both of them but said nothing, continuing to lead the way forward.

Eventually, they reached a wide river and came to a halt. Denki caught his breath and said, "This should be far enough."

Houki’s eyes suddenly narrowed, darting around the dark treeline. "I can smell you... and your dogs."

Kiba appeared across the river, standing directly behind where Houki was looking, his hood casting a deep shadow over his face. While Akamaru quietly lapped up water nearby, three other ninken stepped out from the brush in the exact direction Houki had been facing, completely surrounding them.

_The massive ninken..._ Denki thought, his heart hammering against his ribs. _This must be Kiba of the Inuzuka clan. I couldn't sense his presence at all when we left the village. How long has he been tracking us?_

Houki didn't break eye contact with Kiba. "Now that we are all here, I will brief you on the current situation. The Feudal Lord’s son, Daiku, is embarking on a trip to the Land of Mist. An ANBU team and I intercepted a message containing plans to kidnap him along the way."

Houki's expression grew grimmer. "Under normal circumstances, the ANBU would handle this internally—especially since we have a rogue ninja on our hands."

"A rogue ninja?" Iwabe and Denki blurted out in unison.

"Yes," Houki continued. "But right now, the village's resources are spread incredibly thin. The Hokage needs every available hand on the ground in preparation for a threat that is well beyond my clearance level. We don't know the exact time or how many people will attack, but our mission is simple: we are to escort the prince from a distance without being noticed, and without giving Yutara—the rogue Hyuga clan member—a chance to strike."

Houki looked at each member of the team. "This will be one of our toughest missions to date. Capturing a Hyuga is no simple task. If any single one of us goes one-on-one with Yutara, we will lose. Our mission's success relies entirely on Kiba's sense of smell having a wider range than the Byakugan’s vision, and Denki's technical support. Are we all clear on the details?"


"Don't underestimate me, kid," Kiba scoffed, taking a slow sip from his bottle. "A Byakugan user of Yutara's level can't even make me sweat. Just try to keep up—me and my pack move quickly."

_I’ll finally have a chance to see how a veteran of the Konoha 13 battles in the field,_ Denki thought, growing eager. _I’m almost excited by how much data I can collect for my drone models. But fighting a Hyuga will surely be difficult. I can't rely on cheap tricks, since their X-ray vision can see right through my tech's structural weaknesses._

"My ninken will scout ahead in a parabola," Kiba commanded, his lazy demeanor sharpening into a tactical focus. "We will catch up, verify any suspicious persons, and keep moving. But remember, we are entering foreign borders. We have to be mindful of how we compose ourselves so we don't trigger an international crisis."

"My pack moves in three minutes. We follow right after," Kiba added.

"Three minutes? That should be more than enough time to crush you," Iwabe said, a competitive smirk spreading across his face as he unstrapped his heavy wooden staff and slammed it into a ready position.

"Iwabe! What are you doing?!" Denki yelled, panicking.

"Kiba-san was the very first opponent Lord 7th beat in the Chunin Exams," Iwabe replied, his eyes locked on the veteran. "If I'm going to walk the path of the Seventh, this is a perfect opportunity to test myself against where he started!"

_Is that all I am to these young ninjas?!_ Kiba screamed internally, a comedy-style vein popping on his forehead. _Curse you, Naruto! Always stealing my spotlight!_

Houki frowned, crossing his arms. "Nonsense. We have better things to do than bicker."

"You know what? Fine," Kiba said, his bitter pride taking over. He smirked, cracking his knuckles. "While this isn't exactly conventional, I'll grant your wish. Who am I to stop the younger generation from trying to follow in the Lord 7th's footsteps?"

Weaving a quick hand sign, Kiba yelled, "Shadow Clone Jutsu!"

With a burst of smoke, two perfect clones manifested beside him. "We'll do a quick three-way sparring match. It'll give me a chance to see what you kids can actually do before we hit enemy territory."

_And I'll show these youngsters that I'm still a force to be reckoned with!_ Kiba added in his head, completely fired up.

Denki’s expression instantly flipped from worried to ecstatic. _Amazing! I wonder what kind of data we can harvest from this encounter!_ He eagerly lifted his customized ninja tech suitcase, turning it into combat mode.

Houki sighed, recognizing there was no stopping them now. He unsheathed the broad, heavy knife strapped to his back. "If this is how we are doing things... prepare yourselves."

In an instant, Iwabe and Houki blitzed forward, launching themselves at the real Kiba. Kiba leapt backward in a fluid arc, leading them away from the narrow riverbank to give everyone maximum space to fight.

"Water Style: Water Fountain Jutsu!" Houki roared mid-air.

A massive vortex of pressurized water erupted from the river, crashing down toward Kiba like a tidal wave. Kiba executed a series of rapid backflips to escape the crushing pressure. But just before his feet could touch the dirt, Houki materialized directly in his blind spot, swinging his massive blade with blinding speed.

_Clang!_

Suddenly, the real Houki dropped from the canopy above, his blade slicing down directly toward Kiba’s face. _Got you!_ Houki thought.

But Kiba didn't dodge. With lightning-fast reflexes, he clamped his teeth directly onto the flat of Houki's blade, locking it in place. Catching Houki completely off guard, Kiba twisted his neck with monstrous force, hurling Houki through the air toward a thick oak tree.

Before Houki could even recover his balance, Kiba unleashed his signature technique. "Ninja Art: Beast Human Clone!"
